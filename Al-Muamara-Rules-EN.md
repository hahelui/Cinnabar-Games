## 📖 Game Overview

**Al-Mu'amara (The Conspiracy)** is a strategic social deduction game where players are secretly divided into two factions: **Citizens** trying to maintain order, and **Criminals** working to undermine it from within.

> 🎯 **Core Concept:** Players are assigned hidden roles. Citizens don't know who the Criminals are. Criminals know their teammates. Through rounds of leader selection, voting, and card play, each team tries to reach their victory condition first.

### Game Details

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Players** | 6 to 12 players (each plays from their own phone) |
| **Duration** | 20-40 minutes per game |
| **Genre** | Social deduction / Hidden roles |

### How to Start

1. **Create a Session** — The host creates a game session on their phone and receives a unique game code
2. **Share the Code** — Each player enters the code on their own phone to join the session
3. **Begin the Game** — Once all players have joined, the game begins automatically with role distribution

## 🎭 Secret Roles

At the start of the game, each player receives a secret role. Your role determines your objective, what you know, and how you should play. **Keep your role hidden!**

---

### 🛡️ The Citizen

**Goal:** Protect the City & Uncover the Criminals

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **What You Know** | You do NOT know who the Criminal is. You're watching and analyzing behavior to figure it out. |
| **Your Goal** | Play **5 green cards** to win the game for the Citizens. |
| **What You See** | You can see voting patterns and suspicion reports — pay attention to who is suspected of lying. |

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Use investigation powers wisely — don't reveal your information too quickly. Build trust with other players through observation.

### 💀 The Criminal

**Goal:** You're the Gang Leader — Deceive Everyone

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **What You Know** | You know ALL your teammates from the very beginning of the game. |
| **Your Goal** | Play **6 red cards** to win the game for the Criminals. |
| **What You See** | You can see voting patterns and suspicion reports just like Citizens. |

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Hide your identity and accuse innocent citizens. No one should suspect you! Coordinate with your Assistants secretly.

### 🤝 The Assistant

**Goal:** Secretly Aid the Criminal

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **What You Know** | You know who the Criminal is, but you do NOT know who the other Assistants are. |
| **Your Goal** | Help the Criminal by voting strategically and playing red cards when possible. |
| **Your Challenge** | You must appear as a regular Citizen while secretly working against them. |

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Lie and pretend to be a Citizen so you don't get discovered. Support the Criminal through subtle voting and card play.

## 🔄 Round Structure

Each round follows the same sequence. Understanding this flow is essential to playing well.

### 1. Leader Appointment
The leader role rotates among all alive players. Every round, a new player becomes the Leader.

### 2. Consultant Selection
The Leader chooses any player to be their Consultant — this can't be themselves or the previous Leader/consultant.

### 3. Team Vote
All alive players vote to Approve or Reject the Leader-Consultant team. Majority rules.

### 4. Card Play
If approved, the Leader draws 3 cards, Eliminate 1 , passes to the Consultant, and the Consultant do the same , the remaining card get reveled and played.

### 5. Special Power (Optional)
If a red card is played, the Leader may activate a special power depending on how many red cards have been played so far.

### 6. Advance to Victory
Each played card brings one team closer to their victory condition. The game continues until someone wins.

## 🗳️ Voting Phase

After the Leader selects a Consultant, all alive players vote from their phones. The majority determines the outcome.

after the voting we will display a dialogue showing what each player vote and the result.

| Vote | Result |
|------|--------|
| ✅ **Approve** | Majority Approves → Team plays cards |
| ❌ **Reject** | Majority Rejects → Leader passes, reject count +1 |

### ⚠️ Consecutive Vote Failure (2 Rejects in a Row)

If voting fails twice consecutively (2/2 rejects):
- A **red card is played automatically** for the Criminals
- Any special power on that card is **NOT activated**
- The reject count resets to zero
- **Everyone must approve** the next team — no more rejects allowed!

## 🃏 Card Play — The Heart of the Game

This is where the game is won or lost. The Leader and Consultant work together (or against each other) to play cards.

> **How It Works:**
> 1. The **Leader draws 3 cards**
> 2. The Leader **Eliminate 1 card** and passes to the Consultant
> 3. The Consultant **Eliminate 1 card** and the remaining one get revealed
> 
> The played card determines which team scores!

### Card Types

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| 🟢 **Green Card** | Point for the **Citizens**. Citizens need **5 green cards** to win. |
| 🔴 **Red Card** | Point for the **Criminals**. Criminals need **6 red cards** to win. |

### Card Count by Player Count

| Players | 🔴 Red Cards | 🟢 Green Cards |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| 6-8 | 11 | 6 |
| 9-10 | 13 | 6 |
| 11-12 | 15 | 6 |

## ⚡ Special Powers

Every time a **red card is played**, the Leader may receive a special power. These powers can dramatically shift the game's momentum!

Each time a special power get activated show a dialogue for players of what power got activated and who will use it ( current leader ) but for the leader show the dialogue based on the special power.

### 🔍 Investigation
**Triggered at:** 2nd Red Card

The current Leader chooses a player and secretly reveals their card. The Leader learns whether that player is a **Citizen** or on the **Criminal team**.

> *This is powerful intel — use it to confirm suspicions or clear allies!*

---

### 👑 Leader Selection
**Triggered at:** 4th or 5th Red Card

The current Leader becomes the **next Leader directly**, bypassing the normal rotation. This gives the Leader power to control the game's flow and maintain momentum.

> *Strategic use can keep a trusted player (or a deceptive Criminal) in power.*


---

### 💀 Execution
**Triggered at:** 5th Red Card (9+ players only)

The current Leader **kills a player permanently**! Or they can kill the shortest player (a humorous twist)!

**If the killed player was the Criminal → Citizens win immediately!**

> *This is the ultimate high-stakes power. Execute the wrong person and the Criminals get closer to victory.*

## 🏆 Victory Conditions

### 🛡️ Citizen Victory

Citizens win if either:
- **5 green cards** are played
- The Criminal is **executed** using the Execution power

### 💀 Criminal Victory

Criminals win if:
- **6 red cards** are played

> *This is their ONLY path to victory. They need more cards than Citizens because they have the advantage of knowing their teammates.*

### ⚡ Instant Win Conditions

- If the **Criminal is executed** → Citizens win instantly
- If a **dead player was the Criminal** → Citizens win instantly

## 📋 Additional Rules & Mechanics

### Knowledge System

What players know about each other changes based on player count:

| Players | Criminal Knowledge |
|---------|-------------------|
| 6-10 | Criminals and Assistants know each other completely |
| 11 | Assistants know the Criminal, but not each other |
| 12 | Each Assistant knows the Criminal + only one other Assistant |

### Promotion System (Leader Rotation)

Who can become the next Leader:

The previous consultant cant be chosen by the game as a leader - this prevent the recreation of same leader and consultant over and over again.

| Players | Eligible for Leadership |
|---------|------------------------|
| 6-8 | Only the previous Consultant can't get promoted |
| 9-11 | Previous Consultant AND previous Leader can't get promoted |
| 12 | Last two complete governments (leader + consultant) can't get promoted |

### Dead Players

When a player is eliminated (through Execution or other means):
- They are **eliminated permanently** from the game
- They **cannot speak or vote**
- Their turn is **skipped**
- If they were the **Criminal** → **Citizens win immediately!**

## 🧠 Strategy Tips

### 🛡️ For Citizens

- Watch voting patterns — who agrees with whom?
- If two red cards are played in a row, suspect the Leader and Consultant
- Use investigation powers wisely — don't reveal info too quickly
- Talk to trusted players and exchange information carefully
- Pay attention to who gets selected as Consultant repeatedly
- Reject suspicious teams to force better leadership

### 💀 For Criminals

- Don't act like a natural citizen — blend in, but not too perfectly
- Pass a green card sometimes to build trust with Citizens
- Accuse innocent citizens to create confusion
- Coordinate with your Assistants through voting and card play
- Take advantage of your knowledge advantage
- Use the Execution power carefully — don't expose yourself
